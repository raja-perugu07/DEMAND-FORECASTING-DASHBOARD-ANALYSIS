# DEMAND-FORECASTING-DASHBOARD-ANALYSIS

## Overview

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard developed to analyze retail demand patterns across stores, products, regions, and time periods. The dashboard enables business stakeholders to monitor demand performance, identify high-performing products and stores, and support inventory planning through data-driven insights.

## Business Problem

Retail businesses need to understand how customer demand varies across locations, products, and time in order to optimize inventory allocation and operational planning.

This dashboard helps answer key business questions:

* How does demand change throughout the year?
* Which stores generate the highest demand?
* Which products contribute most to overall demand?
* How is demand distributed across regions?
* Does product pricing significantly influence demand?

## Dataset

**Source:** Kaggle Demand Forecasting Dataset

The dataset simulates retail business operations and includes:

* Demand
* Units Sold
* Units Ordered
* Product Categories
* Store Locations
* Regional Information
* Product Pricing
* Discounts
* Inventory Levels
* Competitor Pricing
* Date-based Transactions

The dataset enables analysis across multiple business dimensions including time, geography, products, and pricing.

## Key Insights

### 1. Demand Fluctuation Throughout the Year

Demand reached approximately 976K units in January before declining sharply in February and later recovering during the middle of the year. A secondary peak occurred around August, followed by stabilization during the final months of the year. This pattern highlights the importance of seasonal demand planning.

### 2. Consistent Store Performance

Store demand remains relatively balanced across locations. Store S002 recorded the highest demand, while the difference between the highest and lowest performing stores was only about 6%, indicating stable operational performance across locations.

### 3. Product Demand Concentration

The top-performing products generated demand levels between 397K and 450K units. Demand is distributed across multiple products rather than relying heavily on a single item, reducing business risk.

### 4. Regional Demand Analysis

The North region contributes approximately 40% of total demand, making it the strongest market. The remaining demand is distributed relatively evenly among South, East, and West regions.

### 5. Pricing Does Not Fully Explain Demand

Analysis shows that lower prices do not automatically generate higher demand. Product demand appears to be influenced by additional factors such as product category, customer preferences, and promotional activities.

## Business Recommendations

* Increase inventory allocation ahead of peak demand periods.
* Prioritize inventory distribution to high-performing regions such as the North.
* Maintain balanced stock levels across top-performing products.
* Evaluate demand drivers beyond pricing alone.
* Monitor seasonal demand patterns for improved forecasting accuracy.
